# Biscotti

Biscotti is a potent, indica-dominant hybrid (roughly 80% indica / 20% sativa) bred by Cookie Fam Genetics, the team behind the Cookies brand and the wider Gelato/Cookies family. It is a designer cross of Gelato #25 and South Florida OG, combining the dessert sweetness and bag appeal of the Gelato line with the heavy, fuel-forward backbone of OG genetics. THC typically lands in the low-to-mid 20s percent (sources range from about 21% to 25%), with negligible CBD under 1%.

The strain is prized for a sweet, creamy, cookie-and-cocoa aroma underlaid with diesel and warm spice, driven by a terpene profile led by caryophyllene and limonene with supporting myrcene and linalool. Effects tend to creep up: an early cerebral, euphoric lift gives way to a deeply relaxing, body-heavy calm, making it a popular late-day or evening smoke for stress, low mood, and unwinding.

Biscotti has become a flagship modern hybrid and a foundational parent for many newer crosses (Biscotti Sundae, Purple Biscotti, Guava Biscotti, and others), cementing it as a sought-after, dispensary-favorite cultivar rather than a mass-market everyday strain.

## Lineage & history

Biscotti was developed by Cookie Fam Genetics (the breeding collective associated with the Cookies brand) in California as part of the prolific Gelato/Cookies dessert-strain lineage. It is most consistently documented as a cross of Gelato #25 and South Florida OG, pairing the sweet, colorful, terpene-rich Gelato side with the dense, fuel-heavy structure of an OG. A minority of secondary sources cite alternative parentage such as Motorbreath x Gelato 41, but the Gelato #25 x South Florida OG lineage is the SeedFinder and Leafly consensus. Since its release, Biscotti has itself become a widely used parent, spawning numerous popular descendants and helping define the modern bakery/dessert flavor trend in cannabis.

## Growing notes

Biscotti is a photoperiod strain best suited to growers with at least some experience; it is not the most beginner-friendly cultivar but is far from the most finicky. Plants stay relatively compact and bushy (often around 100-120 cm indoors), benefiting from topping, defoliation, and a managed canopy to maximize light penetration and bud density. Indoor flowering runs about 8-9 weeks (roughly 56-63 days). Indoor yields are moderate to high, commonly cited in the 400-650 g/m² range under good conditions. As with most OG/Gelato-derived hybrids, attention to airflow and humidity during dense flower formation helps prevent mold, and cooler late-flower nights can coax out purple coloration. Vigor and resistance are average for an exotic modern hybrid.
