# Wedding Cake

Wedding Cake is a potent, indica-dominant hybrid (roughly 60% indica / 40% sativa) bred by Seed Junky Genetics, also known as Triangle Mints #23 and sometimes marketed as Pink Cookies. It is one of the most popular dessert-flavored cultivars on the modern market, prized for its high THC content (commonly testing in the 23-27% range, averaging around 25%) and a rich, tangy-sweet profile reminiscent of vanilla frosting over an earthy, peppery base.

The strain delivers a heavy, relaxing body high paired with a euphoric, mood-lifting head buzz, making it a favorite for evening use and for medical patients seeking relief from pain, insomnia, stress, and appetite loss. Its effects start cerebral and uplifting before settling into deep physical relaxation, a balance that earned it broad mainstream appeal.

Wedding Cake rose to fame in the late 2010s and was named Leafly's Strain of the Year in 2019. Beyond its consumer reputation, it is also valued commercially for dense, trichome-coated buds, reliable yields, and excellent terpene preservation, which has made it a staple parent in countless modern crosses.

## Lineage & history

Wedding Cake was created by Seed Junky Genetics (breeder Jbeezy) by crossing Triangle Kush with Animal Mints. Its lineage is debated within the cannabis community: while the Triangle Kush x Animal Mints cross is the most widely accepted account, some sources claim it descends from Cherry Pie crossed with GSC (Girl Scout Cookies), and others argue Wedding Cake is simply a standout phenotype of Triangle Mints (itself Triangle Kush x Animal Mints), which gave rise to its alternate name Triangle Mints #23. It is also distributed under the name Pink Cookies in some markets. Seed Junky did not widely release it in seed or clone form, so most commercially available Wedding Cake genetics come from other seed banks working from cuts of the original. The strain surged in popularity through the late 2010s and was crowned Leafly Strain of the Year in 2019.

## Growing notes

Wedding Cake performs well both indoors and outdoors but prefers a warm, dry climate and is most often grown indoors where humidity can be controlled to protect its dense, resin-heavy buds from mold. It is regarded as relatively easy to grow and forgiving enough for less experienced cultivators, though plants can roughly double in size during the stretch, so topping and training help manage height and improve light penetration. Indoor flowering runs about 8-9 weeks (56-63 days), producing large yields of approximately 500-650 g/m2 of frosty, trichome-dense flower. Growers value it for trichome density, terpene preservation, and reliable production, but should manage airflow and humidity in late flower given the tight bud structure.
