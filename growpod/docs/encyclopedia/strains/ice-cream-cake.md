# Ice Cream Cake

Ice Cream Cake is a potent indica-dominant hybrid (roughly 75% indica / 25% sativa) bred by Seed Junky Genetics from a cross of Wedding Cake and Gelato #33. It belongs to the celebrated "dessert" lineage of the GSC/Cookies family and has become one of the most sought-after night-time strains for its creamy, sweet flavor and heavy, sedating effects.

THC typically tests in the 20-25% range, occasionally higher, with negligible CBD (under 1%). The high opens with a brief uplifting, euphoric rush that softens stress before settling into deep, bone-melting physical relaxation that often ends in couch-lock and sleep. This makes it a popular choice among medical users seeking relief from pain, insomnia, and anxiety.

Its flavor is the strain's signature: sweet vanilla and sugary cake dough with creamy, slightly nutty and cheesy undertones, backed by a dominant terpene set of limonene, caryophyllene, and myrcene with frequent linalool and a touch of pinene. Dense, frosty buds with deep green and purple hues make it as visually striking as it is flavorful.

## Lineage & history

Ice Cream Cake was created by the California breeder Seed Junky Genetics, the operation behind much of the modern Cookies/Cake dessert lineage. It is the offspring of Wedding Cake (Triangle Kush x Animal Mints) crossed with Gelato #33 (Sunset Sherbet x Thin Mint GSC), both themselves indica-leaning descendants of Girl Scout Cookies. The result is a richly stabilized dessert hybrid that rose to prominence in the late 2010s. A separate, unrelated Ice Cream Cake from Mad Scientist Genetics (Dream Cookie x Cheesecake) also circulates, but the Seed Junky cross is the widely recognized version referenced on Leafly and most dispensary menus.

## Growing notes

Ice Cream Cake flowers indoors in roughly 8-9 weeks (56-63 days) and is harvestable outdoors around early-to-mid October. Indoor yields typically run 450-600 g/m², with outdoor plants capable of up to ~2 kg per plant in ideal conditions. Plants stay relatively short and bushy in classic indica fashion, responding well to topping and low-stress training to open the canopy. It carries moderate resistance to mold and pests but, like other dense-budded Cookies descendants, benefits from good airflow and humidity control late in flower to avoid bud rot. Overall it is a moderate-difficulty grow — forgiving enough for experienced hobbyists but rewarding careful environmental management with frosty, resin-heavy buds.
