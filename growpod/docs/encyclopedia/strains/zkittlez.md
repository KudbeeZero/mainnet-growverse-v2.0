# Zkittlez

Zkittlez is an award-winning, indica-dominant hybrid famous above all for its candy-like, fruity flavor that mirrors the rainbow of sweet, tropical, and berry notes of its namesake confection. Bred in California from Grape Ape and Grapefruit (with a closely guarded third parent), it pairs an exceptionally sweet terpene profile with a moderate, well-balanced THC range typically in the high-teens to mid-twenties percent.

The high is relaxing without being overly sedating: users report a calm, happy, and uplifted body buzz that keeps the mind clear and focused, making it a versatile any-time-of-day strain that leans into evening relaxation at higher doses. CBD is negligible (well under 1%), so its appeal is recreational and terpene-driven rather than therapeutic CBD content.

Cup-winning and highly sought after, Zkittlez is a benchmark "exotic" flavor strain that has spawned countless crosses (Zerbert, Runtz, and more). It rewards growers with dense, colorful buds and generous yields while remaining forgiving enough for less experienced cultivators.

## Lineage & history

Zkittlez was created in California by Terp Hogz (growers Fields and Tony Mendo) in collaboration with 3rd Gen Family's Brandon Parker, with seeds released under the Dying Breed Seeds banner. Its parents are the indica Grape Ape and the citrus-forward Grapefruit, crossed with an undisclosed third strain that the breeders have kept secret. Grape Ape contributes deep, soothing indica relaxation and grape notes, while Grapefruit lends bright, sativa-leaning citrus energy. The strain rose to prominence after sweeping the 2015 Cannabis Cup, winning Best Indica in both San Francisco and Michigan and a top concentrate prize, cementing its reputation as one of the most flavorful and influential modern cultivars.

## Growing notes

Zkittlez is generally considered easy to moderate to grow, thriving both indoors and outdoors and tolerant enough for beginners while rewarding experienced growers who apply training (topping, LST, SCROG) to maximize its potential. Indoors it flowers in roughly 7-9 weeks, producing dense, resinous, often colorful purple-tinged buds and yields around 500-700 g/m². It is a hardy, robust plant ("tough as nails") with good resilience, performing well in warm, sunny Mediterranean-type climates outdoors where harvest typically lands in early-to-mid October. Letting it finish toward week 9 maximizes resin and the signature sweet flavor.
