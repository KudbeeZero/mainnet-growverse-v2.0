# Gushers

Gushers (also marketed as Gusherz, White Gushers, and TK41) is an indica-leaning hybrid (~60% indica) bred by California's Cookie Fam Genetics from a cross of Gelato #41 and Triangle Kush. Named after the fruit-snack candy, it is prized for an intensely sweet, fruity, berry-and-cookie flavor layered over a kushy, earthy base, with some phenotypes leaning more toward gas and spice.

Potency is solid but not extreme, with THC typically landing in the upper teens to low twenties (around 18-24%) and negligible CBD. The high is a balanced hybrid experience that opens euphoric, uplifting, and giggly before settling into deep physical relaxation and a characteristic tingly body sensation, making it a popular evening and stress-relief choice.

Dominant terpenes are caryophyllene (peppery spice), limonene (citrus), and myrcene (herbal/earthy), which together drive its sweet-yet-savory profile. Gushers has become a ubiquitous dispensary staple and the namesake parent of an entire family of "Gusherz" crosses, while still carrying competition pedigree from its Emerald Cup placement.

## Lineage & history

Gushers was developed in the United States by Cookie Fam Genetics (the California breeding collective behind the Cookies/Gelato lineage) by crossing Gelato #41 with Triangle Kush, a Florida-born OG Kush phenotype. The Gelato #41 parent contributes the sweet, dessert-like fruitiness and Cookies-family backbone, while Triangle Kush adds OG gas, structure, and resin. The strain takes its name from the Gushers fruit-snack candy, reflecting its juicy, candy-sweet flavor. It earned a 10th-place finish in the Outdoor (Licensed) category at The Emerald Cup 2018 and has since spawned a large family of "Gusherz" hybrids and pheno-hunts. It is also distributed in feminized seed form by breeders such as Royal Queen Seeds.

## Growing notes

Gushers is a hardy, bushy plant with strong vigor and a manageable structure, making it approachable for beginners while still rewarding experienced growers. Indoors it stays compact (roughly 70-120 cm) and finishes relatively quickly at about 55-65 days of flower, with outdoor harvests in early October. Indoor yields are moderate-to-good at around 450-500 g/m2, with outdoor plants producing up to 650-700 g each. Its sturdy frame and vigorous growth give it decent resilience to common stresses; standard training (topping, light defoliation) helps open the bushy canopy for airflow and even light penetration to the dense, resinous, often colorful buds.
