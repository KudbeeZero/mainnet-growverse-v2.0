# Runtz

Runtz is a rare, exotic balanced hybrid created by crossing Zkittlez with Gelato #33, two cornerstone cultivars of the modern designer-cannabis era. It is celebrated for an intensely sweet, fruit-candy flavor that genuinely resembles a bag of the sugary candy it is named after, paired with resin-drenched buds that range from rich purple to lime green. Potency runs above average, with THC typically in the low-to-high 20s (mid-range around 24%) and negligible CBD.

The high is euphoric and uplifting with a talkative, giggly cerebral onset that gradually develops into a smooth, relaxing full-body warmth, making it popular for socializing and unwinding alike. Its dominant terpenes — caryophyllene, limonene, myrcene and linalool — drive a candy-sweet, citrus-and-berry bouquet with a faint peppery gas finish.

Commercialized through the Cookies/Runtz brand, it became a flagship best-seller and a frequent parent strain used to pass on candy sweetness and dense trichome coverage to new hybrids. Runtz is moderately demanding to grow and prized as an exotic, high-shelf cultivar.</parameter>
<parameter name="history">Runtz was bred in California by a grower known as Nick, who partnered with Long Beach rapper Yung LB and his manager Raybama to release it. It officially debuted at the 2017 Emerald Cup, where it reportedly sold roughly five pounds of flower in under ten minutes. The cross combines Zkittlez — source of its signature sweet fruit-syrup terpene profile and resinous stickiness — with Gelato #33, which contributes structural stability, deep purple coloring and a creamy, gas-forward undertone. Commercialized by the Cookies/Runtz organization, it grew into one of the brand's best-selling and most influential strains, spawning a large family of phenotypes and descendants (White Runtz, Pink Runtz, Tropical Runtz, Runtz #33 and many more).</parameter>
<parameter name="growing">Runtz finishes flowering indoors in about 8–9 weeks (56–63 days), with outdoor harvest typically arriving by mid-October. Indoor yields are medium-to-high, around 400–500 g/m², while outdoor plants can produce 500–600 g per plant in good conditions. It is generally rated intermediate difficulty: not the easiest beginner strain due to its exotic genetics and the care needed to coax out its purple coloring, dense trichome coverage and full candy terpene expression, but manageable for growers with some experience. It responds well to topping and a controlled, slightly cooler late-flower environment that brings out purple hues. Vigor is moderate and resin production is heavy.</parameter>
