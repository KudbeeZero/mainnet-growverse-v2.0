# Sunset Sherbet

Sunset Sherbet (also spelled Sherbert, and commonly shortened to Sherbet) is a popular indica-dominant hybrid created by Bay Area breeder Mr. Sherbinski. It is a cross of the legendary Girl Scout Cookies (GSC) and Pink Panties, inheriting GSC's potent, dessert-like character. THC typically tests in the high-teens to low-twenties (roughly 18-24%), with negligible CBD, placing it firmly in the high-potency tier without being an extreme outlier.

The strain is prized for its sweet, fruity, candy-and-berry flavor over a skunky-citrus and earthy aroma. Its terpene profile is led by caryophyllene, limonene, and myrcene, lending spicy-peppery, citrus, and earthy notes respectively, with supporting humulene and linalool. Effects are full-bodied and relaxing but lifted by a cerebral, happy, uplifting buzz, making it a versatile evening or wind-down strain that remains social and creative rather than fully sedating.

Sunset Sherbet is also a foundational parent in modern breeding, most notably as a parent of Sherbinski's Gelato line, which cemented its influence across contemporary dessert hybrids.

## Lineage & history

Sunset Sherbet was bred in the San Francisco Bay Area by Mario Guzman, better known as Mr. Sherbinski, as a Girl Scout Cookies x Pink Panties cross (Pink Panties itself descending from Burmese Kush and a Florida Kush line). It emerged from the same Bay Area cookies-and-dessert breeding scene that produced GSC and went on to become hugely influential, serving as a parent of Gelato (Sunset Sherbet x Thin Mint GSC). Because it spread widely through clone-only cuts and multiple seed-bank reproductions, several breeders (Seed Junky, Divine Genetics, Pyramid Seeds, Linda Seeds, Barney's Farm) now offer their own versions, and lineage attributions and spellings vary across catalogs.

## Growing notes

Sunset Sherbet is considered moderate difficulty, suited to growers with some experience. It flowers indoors in about 8-9 weeks (roughly 56-65 days) and finishes outdoors in late September to early October. Plants are medium height with dense, fluffy, trichome-coated nugs and dark amber pistils. It performs well both indoors and outdoors and responds to training (topping, LST/SCROG) to even the canopy; indoor yields are moderate, commonly around 400-500 g/m2. It carries decent resilience typical of GSC-derived hybrids but benefits from good airflow and humidity control late in flower to protect the dense, resinous buds from mold.
