# Gelato

Gelato is a slightly indica-dominant hybrid (roughly 55% indica / 45% sativa) born in the Bay Area of California from a cross of Sunset Sherbet and Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookies. It belongs to the celebrated Cookie family and takes its name from its sweet, dessert-like aroma and flavor. Typical THC sits in the 20-25% range, with negligible CBD, placing it well above average potency.

The high comes on quickly and is notably head-forward despite the slight indica lean: an uplifting, euphoric cerebral rush paired with deep physical relaxation that rarely tips into couch-lock. Users report a productive, creative, and focused energy alongside relief from pain, stress, and fatigue, making it popular both recreationally and medicinally.

Gelato's dominant terpene is caryophyllene, supported by limonene, myrcene, and linalool, yielding a flavor profile of sweet berry, citrus, and creamy dessert notes over an earthy, pungent base. It has become one of the most influential modern strains, spawning a large number of named phenotypes (Gelato #33, #41, #45) and serving as a parent to many elite hybrids.

## Lineage & history

Gelato was bred in the San Francisco Bay Area by the Cookie Fam collective, with Mario "Sherbinski" Guzman widely credited as its creator. It descends from Sunset Sherbet crossed with Thin Mint Girl Scout Cookies, both themselves Cookie-family genetics, giving Gelato its characteristic sweet, fruity dessert profile. The strain was selected into several numbered phenotypes (notably #33 "Larry Bird," #41, and #45), each refined for flavor and potency. Since its emergence in the mid-2010s it has become a cornerstone of modern American hybrid breeding and a foundational parent for countless elite crosses.

## Growing notes

Gelato is best suited to indoor or controlled-environment cultivation and is rated moderate in difficulty. Plants reach medium-tall height with a relatively compact structure that does not demand much space. It flowers in about 8-10 weeks (roughly 56-70 days) indoors, producing dense, colorful buds. Indoor yields typically run 500-600 g/m², with outdoor plants yielding 600+ g each. The strain has decent pest resistance but is sensitive to humidity and temperature swings, so stable climate control and attentive feeding reward growers; beginners can succeed in a well-managed tent, but a grow or two of experience helps maximize quality.
