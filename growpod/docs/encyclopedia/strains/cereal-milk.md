# Cereal Milk

Cereal Milk is a high-potency, roughly balanced hybrid bred by the Cookies camp (in collaboration with Powerzzzup Genetics) that first gained popularity around 2019. It crosses Y Life (Girl Scout Cookies x Cherry Pie) with Snowman, a sativa-leaning Cookies phenotype, producing a dessert-style strain famous for its sweet, creamy "leftover cereal milk" and ice-cream nose. Buds are dense and heavily frosted with trichomes.

THC typically tests in the low-to-mid 20s (sources cite about 20-25%), with negligible CBD, placing it firmly among potent modern hybrids. The terpene profile is led by caryophyllene, supported by limonene and humulene, which together temper the sugary sweetness with peppery, citrus, and warm earthy depth.

Effects are commonly described as balanced: an upbeat, happy, sometimes giggly mental lift early on that eases into a relaxed, calming body feel. This makes Cereal Milk a flexible all-day or social strain, and it is also reported for stress, anxiety, and low-mood relief.

## Lineage & history

Cereal Milk emerged from the Cookies (Berner) ecosystem around 2019 and is credited to Cookies in collaboration with Powerzzzup Genetics. Its parents are Y Life (a Girl Scout Cookies x Cherry Pie cross) and Snowman, a sativa-dominant phenotype selected from the Cookies Fam line. The strain rode the wave of dessert-flavored "loud" hybrids that defined late-2010s California cannabis culture, prized for an unusually authentic sweet-cream aroma. It has since been bred and distributed by multiple seed banks (including Royal Queen Seeds, Blimburn, and others) and has spawned descendants such as Zereal and Coconut Milk.

## Growing notes

Cereal Milk is considered approachable for less experienced growers, with moderate overall difficulty and good resistance to common pests and diseases. Plants reach a moderate height (roughly 80-140 cm indoors) with tightly spaced internodes, and flower in about 8-10 weeks indoors, with outdoor harvests around mid-to-late October. Indoor yields are solid at roughly 450-550 g/m². The main caution is its dense, resinous buds, which make humidity control important late in flower to avoid bud rot and mold; otherwise it is forgiving and rewards basic defoliation and airflow management.
