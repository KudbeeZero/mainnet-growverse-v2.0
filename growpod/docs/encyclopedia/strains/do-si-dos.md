# Do-Si-Dos

Do-Si-Dos (often shortened to "Dosi") is a potent, indica-dominant hybrid roughly 70% indica / 30% sativa, bred by the Pacific Northwest's Archive Seed Bank. It crosses a rare, resinous OGKB (OG Kush Breath) phenotype of Girl Scout Cookies with the heavy Face Off OG, producing dense, frosty, purple-tinged buds renowned for their sticky trichome coverage and pungent aroma.

THC typically lands in the 20-30% range, putting Do-Si-Dos firmly among the heavier-hitting modern hybrids, with CBD remaining negligible (generally under 1%). The terpene profile is led by limonene, backed by caryophyllene, linalool, and myrcene, which together give it a sweet-earthy, floral, minty-pine character that turns skunky and diesel-like on the exhale.

The high opens with a fast, in-your-face cerebral buzz that melts into deep, sedating full-body relaxation. It is widely used in the evening for stress, anxiety, insomnia, and chronic pain. The pronounced couch-lock and sleepiness make it better suited to nighttime than daytime functional use.

## Lineage & history

Do-Si-Dos was created by Archive Seed Bank, a breeder based in the Pacific Northwest, by crossing a sought-after OGKB (OG Kush Breath) cut of Girl Scout Cookies with Face Off OG (a Face Off OG bx). The OGKB pheno is a rare, extremely resinous Girl Scout Cookies cut discovered by sifting through large seed populations, prized for its trichome production and potency. Combining it with the OG-heavy Face Off OG yielded a stable, high-THC, indica-leaning strain that quickly became an industry staple in the late 2010s and a popular parent for further breeding (e.g., crosses producing strains like Wedding Cake-adjacent lineages and many "Dosi" hybrids). Numerous seed banks now offer Do-Si-Dos variants, but they trace back to Archive's original work.

## Growing notes

Do-Si-Dos can be grown indoors or outdoors and is considered moderately challenging, rewarding experienced growers who manage feeding and environment carefully. Indoors it flowers in about 8-10 weeks (roughly 56-70 days), with outdoor harvest in late September to early October. It tends to stay relatively compact and bushy, making topping and some defoliation useful to open up the canopy and improve airflow to its dense, resin-heavy colas. Indoor yields commonly fall around 450-550 g/m². The heavy trichome coverage makes it excellent for concentrates but also means humidity and bud-rot must be watched closely during late flower. Cooler night temperatures late in flower can coax out purple hues.
